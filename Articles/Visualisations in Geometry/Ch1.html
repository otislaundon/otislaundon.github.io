Chapter 1 Introduction

 

This document has both a PDF version, and an interactive online version, available at https://otislaundon.com/Articles/Visualisations%20in%20Geometry/main.html. Whilst there are images in the PDF version that show single frames of the interactive visualisations, the reader is highly encouraged to open the interactive version.

 

The Lie groups SO⁢(3) and SU⁢(2), and the relationship between them as groups and their corresponding Lie algebras, are a good showcase for many general concepts encountered in the study of Lie groups. They also find applications in a wide range of fields, from computer graphics [VIN21] to physics [HAM17, NAK03, NAB10]. There are several aspects of these groups for which we create interactive visualisations, which can be accessed in a web browser. These visualisations are embedded into the document as if they are just regular figures, except rather than being still images, they are rendered in real time according to the inputs of the user. We visualise the topologies of SO⁢(3) and SU⁢(2) individually as well as the fact that SU⁢(2) is the universal cover of SO⁢(3), showing how SU⁢(2) maps onto SO⁢(3), how paths in SO⁢(3) lift to paths in SU⁢(2), and that the fundamental group of SO⁢(3) is ℤ/2⁢ℤ. We also visualise the left, right, and conjugation self-actions of SO⁢(3) and SU⁢(2) interactively.

We begin with an introduction to the visualisation technique of domain colouring, typically used for complex functions of one variable, and how it relates to the notion of the graph of a function. We then expand on the technique of domain colouring in a way that uses unordered pairs of colours visualised using checkerboard patterns. This takes us from the 3-dimensional space of colours, to the 6-dimensional space of unordered pairs of colours, which makes it possible to faithfully represent some maps that cannot be using standard domain colouring. Having these techniques at our disposal, as well as standard capabilities of computer graphics for 3D rendering, we then show in detail the geometric, topological, and algebraic properties of SO⁢(3) and SU⁢(2).

A significant amount of work was put into developing a method and workflow for creating a LaTeXdocument which can be compiled to both a PDF and an HTML version, such that the figures present in the PDF version come to life in the HTML version as interactive visualisations. This was achieved through the use of the program LaTeXML [MIL24], with some customisation to allow the images in figures to be replaced with HTML canvases, controlled by JavaScript files.

Throughout this document, we will assume background knowledge knowledge of various areas of mathematics, and use standard definitions from general and algebraic topology from Armstrong [ARM83], Munkres [MUN00], and Hatcher [HAT02], and differential geometry, including manifolds and Lie groups from Hamilton [HAM17] and Hall [HAL15]. Since a significant portion of the content in this document is about Lie groups, some of the basic definitions and constructions used for Lie groups and Lie algebras is included in the appendix 6.
